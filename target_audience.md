The Ideal Structure (Top to Bottom)
Section	Purpose
Hero Section	The main hook and the sign-up box. Must be visible without scrolling.
The "Pain/Solution"	3 bullet points or icons showing exactly what the app fixes.
The "Peek"	A high-quality screenshot or a 10-second silent video loop of the UI.
The Incentive	Tell them why they are waiting (e.g., "Early bird pricing" or "Get username priority").
Final CTA	Repeat the email box at the very bottom.

Mobile-First is Mandatory: Since it’s a mobile app, 80-90% of your traffic will likely be on a phone. Design the mobile view first. If the sign-up button is hidden behind a "read more" or buried at the bottom, your conversion will tank.

    The "Thank You" Viral Loop: The page shouldn't end when they hit "Join." Use the confirmation page to give them a referral link. "Move up 100 spots in line for every friend you invite."

    Avoid External Links: Do not put a full navigation menu (About, Blog, Contact) at the top. You want exactly one exit path: the sign-up button.

    The Golden Rule: If you can explain your app's value in 10 words, go no-scroll. If you need 50 words and a picture, go with a short scroll.

Pro-Tip: The "Scarcity" UX

Since you have a specific number (200), don’t just hide it in text. Use design to make it feel tangible:

    The "Founding Member" Badge: Put a small, stylized  label above your email input field that says "Limited Founding Member Spots Remaining."

    A Simple Counter: If possible, add a small, auto-updating counter (e.g., "142 of 200 spots taken") right below the button. It creates real-time pressure that works wonders for conversion rates.

    The "Why": If you want to be extra transparent, add a tiny line of text below the button: "Why? We want 200 power users to help shape the future of Monelo." It makes people feel like they are being invited to an exclusive club rather than just getting a discount.
